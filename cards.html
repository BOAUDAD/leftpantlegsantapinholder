Cards

Dashboard Recommendation Cards

The dashboard recommendation cards use a material design style layout. The icon in the top right serves as a visual accent that contributes to the meaning of the recommendation, but does not serve as the main focus of the card. Rather, the main focus is the number of account holders that could benefit from the suggested campaigns. The type of campaign is emphasized with bold font for easy noticeability upon scanning.

The recommendation cards use the colors in the palette established for the app, all of which are AA accessible when used with white font at at least 18px in size.

Upon hover, the background color lightens and the cursor changes to a pointer to indicate that the cards are actionable.

Dashboard Activity Cards

The placement of icons on activity cards is consistent with that of other cards in the system. Activity cards span the full width of their container to clearly indicate the order of activities, with the most recent at the top.

Note the different time and date treatments for activities that occurred on the current day, on a previous day within the same year, and during a previous year.

Upon hover, the cursor changes to a pointer to indicate that the cards are actionable.

Cards have an automatic height that responds at smaller screen sizes and prevents the text from getting cut off.

Each activity is represented with a different icon, and categories of activities have different background colors. A green icon background indicates that the campaign has been launched, a teal background indicates that campaign details have been edited, dark pink indicates edits to the campaign audience, deep orange indicates that a campaign has been paused or stopped, and orange indicates a change to the ads.

Audience Behavior Cards

The audience behavior cards have a similar format to the activity cards, but since they do not represent a timeline, they can be displayed in columns to make better use of horizontal screen space.

Because they are displayed side-by-side, the behavior cards require a fixed height in order to maintain an orderly UI. This means that, in situations where the card size is very narrow and there is a lot of paragraph text, the text may overflow the card. We recommend keeping the text concise for this reason, but a gradient has also been applied to fade out the overflow text in an elegant way.

The title is hyphenated so that it wraps and is always visible. Word breaks are inserted manually to overcome Chrome's inability to handle automatic hyphenation.

Dashboard Performance Cards

We believe that the campaign metrics users will consider most important are clickthrough rates and conversion rates; however, we have found in user testing that some marketers consider this too much information to view on a card at one time. Therefore, we've separated clicks and conversions into tabs that that allow the user to see both metrics without overwhelming them with visual information.

Tabs on the card are consistent with our guidelines for tabs throughout the system.

Since each card contains multiple clickable areas (the tabs), there is an explicit link to the full report in the bottom left, an area that material guidelines designates for additional actions. This should help users more quickly understand where to click to see detailed information.

The graph is gray to clarify that it is not actionable.

The highest value listed on the y axis is determined by the largest y value reported on the active tab's graph, and will therefore change depending on the tab selected. All subsequent y values are equal measures apart. Values on the x axis begin with the start date of the campaign and end with the current date, and are also divided into equal measures. Values on the x axis stay the same, regardless of the selected tab.

The performance cards are responsive, allowing them to be easily viewed on all devices and screen sizes. If necessary, the title should truncate rather than span multiple lines. This ensures that cards in the same row are the same height.

Usage Cards

Usage cards follow a format similar to the Dashboard Performance cards, except that the first tab shows an overview of metrics related to the feature or product's usage. Most cards will have three metrics that occupy 4 columns each, while time-based cards (such as Desktop session length) only feature two metrics that occupy 6 columns each.

On screen sizes where three metrics become cramped, we can hide the third metric as shown in the ACH payments example. This allows for the most important information to remain visible, while also maintaining an uncluttered UI.

Metrics that have a positive trend are represented with the hex code #00695c and the "trending up" icon, while negative metrics use the hex code #e64a19 and the "trending down" icon.

The height of the cards should remain the same, regardless of which tab is selected.
