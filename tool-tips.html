Tooltips

Styles

Purpose of tooltips

Tooltips are used very sparingly to give the user additional context about a potential action or piece of information, but are not themselves actionable. They should contain clear, concise explanations that don't require additional information beyond what is on the page to be understood. Tooltip content may contain sentence fragments and need to be terminated with punctuation. Tooltips are not the same as ARIA labels or alternate text, and are not a replacement for any accessibility features.

Usage with other tools

Tooltips may be enabled or disabled by the user, or be replaced by coachmarks depending on the app's context. Since they are meant for clarifying non-obvious contexts (such as when a button is disabled due to permissions), they will often contain different information than coachmarks.

Tooltips should preferably appear below elements, but when a tooltipped element is sufficiently close to the bottom of a viewport, a top tooltip may be used. To keep interfaces as mobile-friendly as possible, avoid using left or right tooltips except when absolutely necessary.

Acceptance Criteria

User testing (maybe we'd link to some definitions and procedures here):

  1. Accurately describes the contex of the operation. (define)
  2. Can be activated by most people. (define)
  3. Is noticeable by most people. (define)

Tech testing (maybe we'd link to some testing procedures and standards here):

  1. Content is not longer than 40 characters.
  2. Appears under or over element depending on location in viewport.
  3. Appears in less than 500 ms on most browsers.
  4. Only one tooltip displays in the page at any time.
  5. Appears on mouseover.
  6. Appears when button is in keyboard focus.
